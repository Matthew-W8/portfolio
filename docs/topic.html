Over commercialization of Video Games

Main Topic

Over time, video games are becoming more and more a product over art. Like movies, they are succumbing, and in my opinion even faster, to the woes of easy money. Whether it be adding product placement or full-on advertisements to interrupt your time.

In-app purchases

Some of my best memories as a child are sitting in the back of my parent's car playing mobile games on my Kindle. After some nostalgic exploration, I concluded that most of them were bad, yet there was something special about them. To me, it's the fact that those games were made to be enjoyed, not a product. Now most mobile games follow a "freemium" format. The game itself is free, however, this leads to it being packed full of ads and in-app purchases, often without an option to just pay to disable ads.

This can create harmful environments where vulnerable people, i.e. those with preexisting conditions that make them more prone to gambling or hoarding, get stuck in loops where they have to obtain everything this game has to offer. This brings to mind the concept of gacha games, but that is a slightly more nuanced subject when compared to virtual currency conversion in these types of games.

While developers must make money someway, most of the ways that do that come off incessantly disrupting or greedy. The best way I have seen it is to provide the option to buy currency to advance faster. While I still think it's not great, it is much more of an option rather than a requirement to access content. The other more player friendly way is the option to watch ads for rewards. Rather than making the advertisement a requirement, let the user decide if they want to watch an ad. I'm more than happy to set my phone down for thirty seconds and not look at the screen if I know I'm getting a reward. It's a win-win scenario. I get rewards, the developers make money, and the advertisers lose money.
